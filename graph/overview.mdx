The Graph is the central workspace in StackMap. It renders your application's infrastructure topology as an interactive node graph, giving you a real-time picture of every service your app depends on and how those services relate to each other. Whether you're onboarding a new team member, debugging an outage, or planning a migration, the Graph lets you see the full picture at a glance.

## What the Graph displays

Every element on the canvas maps to something real in your infrastructure:

- **Service nodes** represent individual services — a DNS provider, an SSL certificate, a database, a payment processor, and so on. Each node is a card that shows the provider logo, service type, account or zone name, current status, and expiry date where applicable.
- **Relationship edges** are the lines connecting nodes. An edge from node A to node B means that A depends on B. For example, your hosting service might depend on your DNS configuration, and your DNS configuration might depend on your domain registrar.

Together, nodes and edges form a dependency map that reflects the true shape of your stack.

## How the Graph is populated

StackMap builds your graph from three sources:

- **Automatic discovery** — StackMap scans connected accounts and detects services, credentials, and relationships without manual input.
- **.env file import** — Paste or upload a `.env` file and StackMap parses the variables to identify services and pre-populate nodes.
- **Manual entry** — Add any service node by hand and draw edges between existing nodes to capture relationships that discovery can't detect automatically.

You can combine all three approaches. Most teams start with an import or discovery pass, then refine the graph manually to fill in gaps.

## Status color coding

Every node carries a status badge that reflects the current health of that service. The color scheme is consistent across the entire Graph:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Healthy" icon="circle-check" iconType="solid" color="#22c55e">
    Green — the service is active and no issues have been detected.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Expiry Warning" icon="circle-exclamation" iconType="solid" color="#f97316">
    Orange — the service has a tracked expiry date that falls within the next 30 days.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Critical or Expired" icon="circle-xmark" iconType="solid" color="#ef4444">
    Red — the service expires within 7 days, has already expired, or StackMap detected an error.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Status is refreshed automatically each time StackMap runs a discovery scan. You can also trigger a manual refresh from the Graph toolbar.
</Note>
