The Graph canvas is built for large, complex infrastructure maps. Once your graph grows beyond a handful of nodes, knowing how to navigate efficiently makes the difference between a frustrating experience and a smooth one. This page covers every way to move around the canvas, rearrange your layout, and stay oriented.

## Zooming

You can zoom in and out using any of these methods:

- **Scroll wheel** — Roll the scroll wheel up to zoom in, down to zoom out. Works anywhere on the canvas background.
- **Pinch gesture** — On a trackpad or touchscreen, pinch two fingers together to zoom out, spread them apart to zoom in.
- **Zoom controls** — Use the **+** and **–** buttons in the toolbar (bottom-left of the canvas) for precise, step-by-step zooming.
- **Keyboard shortcut** — Hold **Ctrl** (Windows/Linux) or **Cmd** (macOS) and scroll the mouse wheel to zoom.

<Tip>
  Double-click a node to zoom directly to it and center it in the viewport — useful when you know which service you want to inspect but can't find it on a busy canvas.
</Tip>

## Panning

To move across the canvas without changing the zoom level, click and drag on any empty area of the canvas background. Your cursor changes to a grab hand to confirm you're dragging the canvas rather than a node.

<Note>
  Clicking and dragging on a node card moves the node itself, not the canvas. To pan, make sure you click on the background between nodes.
</Note>

## Selecting nodes

You can select nodes to act on them individually or as a group:

- **Single select** — Click a node to select it and open its detail panel.
- **Multi-select** — Hold **Shift** and click additional nodes to add them to your selection. Selected nodes are highlighted with an outline.
- **Select all** — Press **Ctrl+A** (Windows/Linux) or **Cmd+A** (macOS) to select every node on the canvas at once.

With multiple nodes selected, you can drag them as a group to reposition them together, or press **Delete** to remove them all at once.

<Warning>
  Pressing **Delete** with multiple nodes selected removes all selected nodes and their connected edges. Confirm your selection before deleting.
</Warning>

## Dragging nodes to rearrange

Click and drag any node card to move it to a new position on the canvas. Your custom positions are saved automatically — StackMap remembers your layout between sessions.

Rearranging nodes is useful for:

- Grouping related services together visually (for example, all DNS and SSL nodes in one cluster)
- Reducing edge crossings to make the dependency flow easier to read
- Making room for newly added nodes

## Auto-layout

If your graph becomes cluttered or you want to start fresh with positioning, click the **Auto-layout** button in the Graph toolbar. StackMap recalculates node positions using an automatic layout algorithm that minimizes edge crossings and groups connected nodes.

<Note>
  Auto-layout overwrites any manual positioning you have done. If you want to preserve your current layout, note your arrangement before clicking — there is no undo for a layout reset.
</Note>

## The minimap

The minimap is a small overview panel in the bottom-right corner of the canvas. It shows a bird's-eye view of your entire graph, with a shaded rectangle indicating the portion currently visible in your viewport.

You can interact with the minimap in two ways:

- **Click** anywhere on the minimap to jump the viewport to that area of the graph.
- **Drag** the shaded viewport rectangle on the minimap to pan the canvas smoothly.

To toggle the minimap on or off, click the **Minimap** button in the Graph toolbar.

<Tip>
  On large projects with dozens of nodes, keep the minimap open. It's the fastest way to jump between clusters of services — especially when you're chasing a dependency chain across a sprawling graph.
</Tip>

## Keyboard shortcuts reference

| Action | Windows / Linux | macOS |
|---|---|---|
| Zoom in / out | Ctrl + Scroll | Cmd + Scroll |
| Select all nodes | Ctrl + A | Cmd + A |
| Delete selected node(s) | Delete | Delete |
| Multi-select (add to selection) | Shift + Click | Shift + Click |

<Info>
  Keyboard shortcuts are active whenever the Graph canvas has focus. If a shortcut isn't responding, click once on the canvas background to give it focus, then try again.
</Info>
