Even when everything is configured correctly, you may occasionally run into unexpected behavior. This page walks you through the most common issues StackMap users encounter, why they happen, and exactly what to do about each one.

<Accordion title="Domain discovery found fewer services than expected">
  ### Cause

  Domain discovery works by scanning DNS records (A, AAAA, CNAME, MX, TXT, NS) and inspecting SSL certificate Subject Alternative Names. It can only surface services that have a DNS footprint — meaning they're referenced by a DNS record attached to your domain. Services that communicate over direct IP addresses, private networks, or have no public DNS entry won't appear in a domain scan.

  ### Fix

  Supplement the automated scan using one or both of the following methods:

  - **Import your `.env` file** — StackMap's client-side parser reads your environment variable key names and maps them to known providers, surfacing services that DNS scanning can't detect.
  - **Add services manually** — Click **Add Service** in the graph toolbar and enter the service details directly.

  <Tip>
    Using all three discovery methods together — domain scanning, `.env` import, and manual entry — gives you the most complete picture of your infrastructure.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="A service node shows 'Checking' indefinitely">
  ### Cause

  When StackMap discovers or adds a service, it runs a verification pass to confirm the service's status and fetch metadata such as SSL expiry dates. During periods of high load, this verification may take a few extra minutes to complete.

  ### Fix

  1. Wait **2–3 minutes**, then refresh the page. The status should resolve once the verification completes.
  2. If the node still shows "Checking" after several minutes, navigate to **Project Settings → Discovery** and re-trigger the discovery scan for that project to start a fresh check.
  3. If the issue persists after re-triggering, contact [StackMap support](mailto:support@stackmap.io) with your project ID so the team can investigate.

  <Note>
    A node stuck on "Checking" does not mean the service is down — it only means StackMap hasn't finished fetching its metadata yet.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The .env parser didn't detect my service">
  ### Cause

  StackMap's `.env` parser identifies providers by matching your environment variable key names against a built-in fingerprint database. If your key name uses a non-standard naming convention — or if the provider simply isn't in the database yet — the parser won't be able to map it to a known service.

  ### Fix

  1. **Add the service manually** — click **Add Service** in the graph toolbar, enter the service name and type, and save. The node will appear on your graph immediately.
  2. **Submit a provider request** — open a support ticket and include the key name pattern (e.g., `MY_PROVIDER_API_KEY`) and the provider's name. The StackMap team will add it to the fingerprint database so future `.env` imports detect it automatically.

  <Info>
    Your secret values are never transmitted. When you submit a provider request, you only need to share the key name pattern — not the value.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Expiry date appears incorrect">
  ### Cause

  StackMap reads expiry dates from the most recent WHOIS or SSL scan stored in its database. If the scan data is stale — for example, if a domain was recently renewed but StackMap hasn't re-scanned since — the displayed expiry date will reflect the old value.

  ### Fix

  1. Go to **Project Settings → Discovery**.
  2. Click **Re-trigger Scan** for the affected domain or service.
  3. Wait for the scan to complete (typically under 60 seconds), then return to the graph. The expiry date will update automatically once the scan finishes.

  <Tip>
    After renewing a domain or SSL certificate, always re-trigger a scan in StackMap so your graph and alerts reflect the new expiry date right away.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I can't see the graph — it's blank">
  ### Cause

  A blank graph means the project has no services associated with it yet. This happens most often with newly created projects where discovery hasn't been run, or when a domain scan completed but found no resolvable DNS records.

  ### Fix

  Populate your graph using one or more of these methods:

  - **Run domain discovery** — enter your app's primary domain in the discovery panel and start a scan.
  - **Import a `.env` file** — drag and drop your environment file onto the import panel to extract service key names.
  - **Add a service manually** — click **Add Service** in the toolbar and fill in the service details.

  Once at least one service is added, the graph will render and you can continue building out your infrastructure map.

  <Warning>
    If you ran domain discovery and the graph is still blank, double-check that the domain you entered resolves publicly. Private or intranet-only domains won't return DNS results.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Graph layout is hard to read on large projects">
  ### Cause

  As you add more services, nodes can overlap or cluster together, especially if you've rearranged some manually. Large graphs with many connections can become difficult to navigate at the default zoom level.

  ### Fix

  - Click the **Auto-layout** button in the graph toolbar to instantly reset all nodes to a clean, algorithm-driven arrangement. Your previous manual positions will be replaced.
  - Use the scroll wheel or pinch gesture to **zoom out** and get a bird's-eye view of the full graph.
  - Open the **minimap** in the bottom-right corner of the graph to see a scaled overview and click to jump to any area of the graph quickly.

  <Tip>
    After running auto-layout, you can still drag individual nodes to fine-tune positions. StackMap saves your layout automatically.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I forgot my password">
  ### Cause

  Passwords are hashed and cannot be retrieved — only reset.

  ### Fix

  1. Go to the [StackMap login page](https://app.stackmap.io/login).
  2. Click **Forgot password?** below the sign-in form.
  3. Enter the email address associated with your account and submit the form.
  4. Check your inbox for a password reset email and follow the link inside. The link expires after 60 minutes.

  If you don't receive the email within a few minutes, check your spam folder. If it still doesn't arrive, contact [StackMap support](mailto:support@stackmap.io).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="OAuth integrations are not available">
  ### Cause

  OAuth integrations with Cloudflare, Vercel, and GitHub are a **Phase 2** feature and have not been released yet. If you see a disabled or greyed-out OAuth option in the UI, this is expected — the feature is coming soon.

  ### Fix

  In the meantime, you can achieve similar coverage using the available discovery methods:

  - **Domain discovery** — surfaces Cloudflare-proxied domains, Vercel deployments, and GitHub Pages via DNS and SSL scanning.
  - **`.env` import** — detects Cloudflare, Vercel, and GitHub API keys by key name pattern, even without an OAuth connection.
  - **Manual entry** — add any service directly to your graph with as much or as little detail as you have.

  <Info>
    When OAuth integrations launch in Phase 2, you'll be able to connect your Cloudflare, Vercel, and GitHub accounts directly to StackMap for deeper, automated discovery.
  </Info>
</Accordion>
