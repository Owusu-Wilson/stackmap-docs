title: "Start Mapping Your Infrastructure with StackMap in Minutes"
sidebarTitle: "Quickstart"
description: "Set up your first StackMap project, run domain discovery, and explore a live interactive graph of every service your app depends on."
---

StackMap turns your app's infrastructure into a live, interactive graph — no agents to install, no manual inventory spreadsheets. The fastest way to get started is domain discovery: give StackMap your domain and it automatically scans DNS records, WHOIS data, and SSL certificates to build your first graph.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Create your account">
    Go to [app.stackmap.io](https://app.stackmap.io) and sign up. You can register with an email address or continue with Google. Once you confirm your email, you land on your empty project dashboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new project">
    Click **New Project** in the top-right corner. Enter a project name that matches the app you want to map — for example, `acme-production` or `my-saas-app`. Your project name is only visible to you and is used to organise multiple graphs in your dashboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose an onboarding method">
    StackMap gives you three ways to populate your graph today. Select **Domain Discovery** for the fastest results.

    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Domain Discovery" icon="magnifying-glass" href="/projects/domain-discovery">
        Enter your root domain (e.g. `acme.io`). StackMap runs WHOIS, DNS, and SSL scans automatically in the background. **Recommended for first-time setup.**
      </Card>
      <Card title=".env Parsing" icon="file-code" href="/projects/env-import">
        Paste or upload your `.env` file. StackMap detects provider key names and maps them as service nodes. Secret values never leave your browser.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Manual Entry" icon="plus" href="/projects/manual-entry">
        Add any service by hand — choose a provider, enter account details, and place the node directly on the graph. Useful for services with no public DNS footprint.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    <Tip>
      **Your secrets stay on your device.** When you use .env parsing, StackMap processes the file entirely in your browser. Only key *names* and their detected provider are sent to StackMap's servers — secret values are stripped before anything leaves the page.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for discovery to complete">
    After you submit your domain, StackMap queues a background job that runs DNS record resolution, WHOIS lookups, and SSL certificate polling. This typically completes in **under 60 seconds** for most domains.

    A progress indicator appears on the project screen. You don't need to keep the tab open — StackMap will finish the scan and the graph will be ready when you return.

    <Info>
      The number of services discovered depends on how many subdomains, DNS records, and referenced third-party providers are associated with your domain. Complex apps with many microservices or integrations may take a little longer.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore your graph">
    Once discovery finishes, click **Open Graph** to enter the interactive canvas. Every detected service appears as a node — drag nodes to rearrange the layout, scroll to zoom, and pan by clicking and dragging the canvas background.

    A few things worth checking first:

    - **Service cards** show each service's logo, type, linked account, and current status.
    - **Edges** connect services that have a detected relationship (for example, a Vercel deployment pointing to a custom domain).
    - **Expiry badges** flag any domain or SSL certificate that is approaching its renewal date.
    - **Minimap** in the bottom-right corner gives you a bird's-eye view of your full graph.

    <Note>
      Don't see a service you know exists? Use **Add Service** in the graph toolbar to add it manually, or paste a `.env` file to pull in additional integrations.
    </Note>
  </Step>

</Steps>
